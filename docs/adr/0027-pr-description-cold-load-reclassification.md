# 27. pr-description failure is cold-load, not a generation stall — retire its flaky_on_models entry

Date: 2026-06-28

## Status

Accepted. Supersedes the `pr-description`-specific premise of ADR 0012 (the worked example of the `flaky_on_models` gate) and ADR 0013 Topic E (pr-description stays hand-written). The `flaky_on_models` gate mechanism (ADR 0012) and the `release-note` / `long-thread-distillation` entries are unaffected.

## Context

Since the 2026-05-24 `flaky_on_models` gate shipped, every `pr-description` call on this MLX host has exited 4 — the gate refuses the recipe whenever the resolved prose-tier model matches `qwen3.6:35b` or `qwen3-next:80b`. The gate rested on a premise accumulated across 2026-05-10/11/12/13 and recorded in ADR 0012 and ADR 0013 Topic E: that 35B-class prose-tier models *generation*-stall on recipe-shaped prompts, "hanging six to ten minutes producing the 2–3 KB of structured markdown a PR body needs, regardless of input size." On that premise the failure was classified as structural (the gate's exit-4 domain, "this model class cannot do this recipe") rather than dynamic (the canary's exit-3 domain, "the model did not respond in time").

A controlled re-measurement on 2026-06-28 — the `DELEGATE_FORCE_FLAKY=1` "override once and measure" step that both ADR 0012 and ADR 0013 Topic E explicitly prescribe as the reopening path — falsified that premise. Seven models were probed with a faithful 3.2 KB recipe-shaped prompt (`think:false`, `temperature:0`), each warmed with a 1-token call before the full generation was timed. The production-path model, `mlx-community/Qwen3.6-35B-A3B-8bit` — the very model the gate refuses — emitted a grade-A, correctly-shaped PR body in 5.7 seconds once warm. The only slow part was a one-time 77-second cold-LOAD. The other candidates also returned in single-digit-to-teens seconds once warm, though shape quality varied rather than being uniformly grade A: qwen3-coder:30b, qwen3.5:27b, and gemma4:31b-it graded A; gemma4:latest dropped the Test-plan section (grade C); and the same 35B weights on the Ollama backend dropped the Summary section (grade D) on the single sample. That spread is a shape/taste signal the gate never measured — not the structural generation failure it assumed. The earlier 2026-05 timeouts measured wall-clock from a cold or memory-evicted state — a 35B prose model and an 80B long-context model thrashing each other out of VRAM — and mis-attributed the disk-load latency to generation.

The decisive corroboration is internal and predates the probe. `commit-message` dispatches through the identical 35B/prose/MLX path with no `flaky_on_models` block and has logged 294 exit-0 successes, alongside 15 transient exit-3 canary cold-load stalls. If the 35B genuinely could not generate recipe-shaped prompts, those 294 successes could not exist, and the 15 exit-3 rows show the cold-load failure is real but transient and self-recovering — exactly what the canary was built to absorb. Under ADR 0012's own evidence convention, which requires each entry to be a falsifiable structural claim anchored in measurement, the `pr-description` entry is now a misclassification: the structural claim is false, and the residual failure belongs to the canary.

Warm output *quality* is a separate axis the probe does not settle. The recipe's historical 45% HIT (5 HIT / 6 MISS, the worst in the library) measured May-era output, and the gate's own design froze verdict collection after 2026-06-18. To avoid trading a loud, observable cold-load failure for a silent quality regression, faithfulness was checked once on a genuinely novel diff: shape-anchored on an unrelated dependabot PR and asked to describe a dashboard-panel fix, the warm 35B produced a shape-perfect body that named the real files and the real bug with zero bleed from the anchor (recorded as a HIT, ts 2026-06-28T20:46:14Z). One novel-diff HIT is a floor, not a quality re-validation.

## Decision

Retire the `flaky_on_models` block from `prompts/pr-description.md` only, and reclassify the failure from structural (exit 4) to dynamic (exit 4 → exit 3 / cold-load). Absorb the cold-load with the existing knob — `DELEGATE_PREFLIGHT_TIMEOUT=90` on a cold MLX 35B host so the canary tolerates the load window, and/or keeping the model resident (Ollama `keep_alive`, or `mlx_lm.server` holding the last model). No `pick-model.sh`, `delegate.sh`, `PROSE_PREFS`, or test change: the gate mechanism is already backwards-compatible for recipes without the block, and the shared prose tier that `commit-message` depends on for its 294 runs is left byte-identical.

Remove, model-replace, and a per-recipe routing field were all rejected. Removal is wrong because the gate suppressed live demand — seven 2026-06 refusals span three distinct projects — and the recipe works once warm; the verify-usage-before-archiving discipline says default-to-keep when usage is non-zero. A gemma4 model-replace is the least portable option: gemma4 is Ollama-only on this host, sits behind the installed giants in the backend-agnostic `PROSE_PREFS` substring list, and there is no per-recipe `tier:`/`model:` frontmatter field, so routing only this recipe to it would need new shared-code machinery and risk the `commit-message` path. The 35B is the highest-quality option on the production backend anyway (grade A warm), so there is no capability reason to swap it.

This reclassification is deliberately scoped to `pr-description`, the only recipe re-measured. `release-note` and `long-thread-distillation` carry their own `flaky_on_models` entries on the same model class; whether the same cold-load reclassification applies to them is an open question that requires its own override-and-measure before any of their entries are touched. They are left in force.

## Consequences

`pr-description` is usable again on 35B-class MLX hosts: the first cold call needs `DELEGATE_PREFLIGHT_TIMEOUT=90` and warm calls return in seconds, and on hosts where the prose tier resolves to a smaller model the recipe was never gated and continues to work. The `flaky_on_models` convention gains a worked example of its full lifecycle — added on measured evidence, then retired via a superseding ADR when fresh measurement falsified the claim — which `prompts/README.md` Convention 4 now teaches as the model for handling a falsified entry rather than silently ripping a gate out.

The principal residual risk is warm quality. The gate retirement is on probation: the next ~10 real `pr-description` calls must record a HIT/MISS via `delegate-feedback.sh` so the recipe re-enters the calibration loop the gate had frozen, and if its hit-rate does not clear the library floor within that window, removal is back on the table — this time on an honest taste signal rather than a misclassified infrastructure failure. A specific facet of that probation: the removal un-gates the recipe on Ollama-prose-35B hosts too, where the single probe sample dropped the Summary (grade D); the cross-backend shape variance is real and is precisely what the HIT/MISS re-measurement now tracks, rather than being pre-empted by a structural refusal. A secondary risk is that raising `DELEGATE_PREFLIGHT_TIMEOUT` is per-call here rather than a global default, so a caller who forgets it on a cold host will see an exit-3 canary stall with a clear "raise the timeout" message — a loud, recoverable failure, not the silent multi-minute hang of the original report. This mirrors ADR 0026's lesson from the same week: fix the recipe, not the backend, and keep the failure observable rather than archiving the signal that would catch it.
