Second baseline. Closes the four open Phase 7 rigour items from the 2026-04-28 run: 3 reps per cell with mean ± stdev, mechanical T3 scoring via `experiments/score-t3.sh`, single regime (sequential with `ollama stop` between models), and a dated T3 fixture (`task-3-merge-patterns-2026-04-28.txt`) so future baselines can ship their own without overwriting this one. Inputs and prompts under `experiments/fixtures/`, raw outputs under `experiments/results/raw/`. All runs from a single workstation (Apple M5 Max, 128 GB unified memory). Total wall: 1h46m (11:16:35Z → 13:02:38Z), one model resident at a time, `ollama stop` plus 2-second pause between models.

## Scoring

T1 and T2 scored by hand from the OUTPUT block of each rep (T1: number of correct CLAIM verdicts out of 4; T2: 1 if the final answer is `CLEAN`, 0 otherwise). T3 scored mechanically by `experiments/score-t3.sh`: for each `CONCERN | PATTERN` line, look up `PATTERN` as a literal substring in the dated T3 fixture; per-rep score is `cited / claimed`, per-cell score is mean across reps. The mechanical scorer replaces the human "real / plausible / hallucinated" rubric used in 2026-04-28 — it removes author bias and is reproducible.

## Findings

T1 is uniform at 4 / 4 across all 15 cells. The closed-form prompt with explicit format spec and four claims with single ground-truth answers is solved by every model regardless of size or tier. This matches the 2026-04-28 finding and is now stable at N=3 per cell.

T2 separates the field along the same line as 2026-04-28. Four of five models return the requested single-token `CLEAN` on every rep. `qwen3-next:80b-a3b-instruct-q8_0` returns `INCONSISTENT` on every rep (0/3) and produces a long content review claiming the SNP climate position value is inconsistent with its stance and quote text — exactly the off-prompt content-review failure observed in the original baseline. The prompt explicitly asks for *structural* deviations only and says "do not invent issues"; the 80B model interprets it loosely and goes off-prompt with high consistency. This is the same regression and confirms it is a stable behaviour, not a one-shot artefact.

The new 122B `premium-general` entry passes T2 cleanly (3/3 `CLEAN`). The off-prompt regression appears to be specific to `qwen3-next:80b`, not a generic large-model trait.

T3 is where the new mechanical scoring earns its place. The headline ordering is: `qwen3-next:80b` (0.75) > `gemma4:31b` (0.71) > `qwen3.6:35b` (0.36) > `phi4-reasoning` (0.32) > `qwen3.5:122b` (0.16). The two important observations are about claim *volume* and *variance*.

`qwen3-next:80b` produced exactly 4 claims per rep on T3 — the cap from the prompt — and 3 of those were supported by the fixture every time. Stdev 0.00. It followed the constraint and stayed conservative. That same trait that made it fail T2 (going long, going off-prompt) is here inverted: short, on-prompt, stable. Worth noting: the 4-claim cap that the prompt requested was respected only by this model.

`qwen3.5:122b` produced 18 / 31 / 32 claims across the three reps — well over the 4-claim cap — with 0, 12, and 3 of them supported respectively. The 122B model ignored the constraint and emitted volume of mostly unsupported concerns. Mean 0.16, the worst of the matrix. This extends the 2026-04-28 finding ("the largest installed model was the worst on inference tasks") into a new size class: at Q4_K_M, the 122B is strictly worse on T3 than the 31B `gemma4`.

`phi4-reasoning:plus` has the highest stdev in the matrix at 0.31 (mean 0.32, range 0.08–0.77). One rep cited 49 of 64 — close to `gemma4`'s consistency. Another cited 2 of 26. Reasoning models with chain-of-thought are unstable run-to-run, and the single-shot 2026-04-28 score of 0.06 was apparently sampled from the bad end of the distribution; the truth is "wide-variance, sometimes great, sometimes near zero". The skill body's "treating long output as confidence" warning is empirically validated by this distribution.

Across all five models, only `qwen3-next:80b` came close to treating `NONE` as a viable answer (its 4-claim runs are right at the cap with 3 supported — close to a refusal posture). Every other model felt obliged to fill 18+ claim slots.

## Implications for tier routing

The 2026-04-28 ordering still holds and the new data sharpens, rather than overturns, every recommendation.

`prose`: keep `qwen3.6:35b` ahead of `qwen3-next:80b`. The T2 INCONSISTENT regression on the 80B is now confirmed at N=3 — it is a stable behavioural trait, not a one-shot. Do **not** add `qwen3.5:122b` to the `prose` preference list; its T3 failure mode (high claim volume, low support rate, ignored constraint) is exactly the pattern the skill warns users away from.

`reasoning`: keep `phi4-reasoning:plus` for closed structured tasks where chain-of-thought wall time is acceptable. T1 and T2 are perfect; T3 wide variance is a known limitation rather than a disqualifier — the tier exists for closed extraction work and that is where it shines.

`long-context`: unchanged. `qwen3.6:35b` continues to be the right choice.

`premium-general`: the existing `qwen3.5:122b-a10b-q4_K_M` entry stays as an *explicit opt-in*, but the SKILL.md framing should be sharpened — this is **not** a "deeper prose" upgrade over the standard `prose` tier. It is a model that produces more output and ignores cap constraints. For tasks where the user wants exhaustive enumeration and is committed to verifying every claim, it has a place; for default prose work, the 35B is empirically better. The tier name "premium-general" risks suggesting it is a quality upgrade over `prose`; future SKILL.md edits should reflect that it is a depth/verbosity opt-in with a known hallucination tradeoff.

The bigger conclusion across both baselines is now stable: *prompt scope* and *constraint adherence* dominate *model size*. T1 (4/4 on every model on every rep), T2 (binary pass/fail driven by whether the model goes off-prompt), and T3 (hallucination rate inversely correlated with claim cap adherence) all point at the same finding. The SKILL.md "Prompt scope — closed vs open" section captured this from the first baseline; this baseline confirms it at N=3 per cell with a 122B data point added.
